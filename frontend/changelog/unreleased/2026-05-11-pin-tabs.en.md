---
title: Pin tabs — sticky leftmost, survive bulk-close
date: 2026-05-11
---

Right-click any tab → **Pin tab**. The pinned tab snaps to the left
edge of the strip and stays there. A small pin icon appears next to
the method indicator on every pinned tab, so the boundary between
pinned + unpinned is obvious at a glance.

Pin semantics match Chrome / Safari:

- **Sticky leftmost** — toggling pin moves the tab to the boundary
  between pinned and unpinned regions.
- **Survive bulk-close** — both **Close others** and **Close tabs to
  the right** skip pinned tabs.
- **Closeable** — explicit close (✕, middle-click, ⌘W) still closes
  pinned tabs.
- **Right-click → Unpin tab** drops the pin + sends the tab back to
  the right of the boundary.

Useful for: keeping your auth / health-check / staging-base-URL
tabs always in view across long debug sessions.

Pinning persists across reloads alongside the rest of the tab
state — re-launch the app and your pinned tabs are still there.
